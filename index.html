Bio

I am a newly minted PhD in Public Policy and Administration from Bocconi University, Milan. My recent work has focused on the influence of public transparency on citizens' trust in government, using experimental and computational methods. I have a wide range of research interests, including the causes and consequences of political behavior, public opinion, and urban sustainability.

I was born in Buenos Aires, where I studied Sociology and worked in the National Ministry of Justice and the Judiciary. In 2016, I moved to New York to study International Affairs at The New School. During this time, I gained applied research experience with the Global Urban Futures Team and the Marron Institute of Urban Management at NYU.

In my free time, I enjoy reading, watching movies, playing chess, working out, and experimenting with large language models.
